Day-3 ## What is Salesforce CRM?

Salesforce is a cloud-based **Customer Relationship Management (CRM)** platform used to manage customers, sales, leads, and business processes.
## Free Practice Environment
### Developer Edition

* Free version of Salesforce for learning and practice
* Includes most Salesforce tools and features
* Has limited storage compared to paid versions
---
## Navigating Salesforce
### App Launcher (Waffle Icon)

* Located at the top-left corner
* Used to open different apps like:
  * Sales App
  * Service App
  * Marketing App

### Home Dashboard
Provides a quick overview of:

* Tasks
* Deals
* Sales performance
* Pipeline progress
---
## Lead Management

### What is a Lead?
A lead is a potential customer or business opportunity.

### Creating Leads
You can add:

* Name
* Company
* Phone number
* Email
* Lead status

### Updating Lead Status

Example statuses:
* Open
* Working
* Qualified
* Converted
---
## Lead Conversion

### What Happens During Conversion?
When a lead becomes a real customer opportunity, Salesforce automatically creates:

#### Account
* The company or organization

#### Contact
* The person from that company

#### Opportunity
* The deal or sale being tracked
---
## Tasks and Collaboration

### Task Management

* Create tasks with due dates
* Track follow-ups and meetings
* Sync with calendar

### Files Section
* Store and share documents
* Similar to Google Drive
---
## Dashboards and Reports

### Custom Dashboards
* Create charts and graphs
* Track sales and team performance
* Display important business metrics
---
# Important Salesforce Terms

| Term        | Meaning                 |
| ----------- | ----------------------- |
| Lead        | Potential customer      |
| Account     | Company/business        |
| Contact     | Person from the company |
| Opportunity | Potential deal/sale     |
| Dashboard   | Visual business data    |
| Task        | Follow-up activity      |
